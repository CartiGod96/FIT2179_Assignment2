The NBL Performance Frontier

A Spatial and Statistical Study of the 2025-2026 Season

Data Visualisation Portfolio by Liam Fowler

Executive Summary & Portfolio Context

Modern basketball analytics has evolved past raw box scores, shifting toward efficiency-driven spatial metrics and possession-adjusted tracking data. This portfolio evaluates the competitive landscape of the National Basketball League (NBL) across its 2025-2026 campaign. By mapping the performance frontiers of franchises and individual athletes, these interactive components dissect the core factors driving success: team efficiency, geographic constraints, defensive workloads, and spatial shot distribution.

Through dynamic filtering and multi-layered visual encodings, this study converts dense tracking records into an exploratory tactical interface—unveiling how team structures navigate travel taxes and paint security architectures to forge title runs.

1. Ranks and Geography

Competitive Velocity

The round-by-round volatility of the NBL ladder captures mid-season surges and functional collapses. Clicking a franchise badge directly in the legend filters clutter out to reveal its path.

The Travel Tax

Proportional markers locate team stadia across Australia and New Zealand. Bubble sizes correspond directly to crowd capacities, visualizing how physical home environments shape competitive advantages.

2. Team Efficiency Frontiers

The Possession Frontier

Points Per Possession (PPP) bypasses raw scoring columns distorted by fast play styles. The top-left quadrant isolates the title core—franchises creating maximum output while restricting opponents.

True Shooting Profiles & Point Differentials

Evaluating the underlying mechanics of scoring efficiency. The left chart unpacks Effective Field Goal percentages (eFG%) across squads, while the right displays net scoring margins across home and away splits.

3. Advanced Player Analytics Suite

Workloads & Assists vs. Points

This profile charts playmaker table-setters against volume scoring anchors. Bubble sizes reflect game durations (MPG). Use the interactive franchise dropdown selector to evaluate internal roster utility distributions.

Paint Security Matrix

Defensive mapping pits blocks against Advanced Rim Recovery (ARR). Use the range slider at the bottom to filter minimal workloads, or click a legend badge to track a single team's interior wall.

Roster Execution Matrices: FIC vs. PIE Metrics

A direct side-by-side diagnostic comparison of holistic impact metrics. Floor Impact Counter (FIC) values raw box-score productivity, whereas Player Impact Estimate (PIE) gauges the exact percentage of game events a player commands.

4. High-Fidelity Shot Tracking Geography

Spatial Court Density and Shot Selection Architecture

Mapping every attempt across the hardwood workspace. This spatial dataset evaluates specific high-value efficiency hubs, contrasting mid-range frequency drop-offs against high-density rim and corner-three distributions.
