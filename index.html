Chapter 02

League Context & Environmental Factors

Competitive Velocity

The landscape of the NBL is constantly changing, and so too are the great teams that top the ladder every year. Teams trade, go through slumps or in rare cases have late mid season surges, with this graphic filtering that all out and looking solely at how the season ends. Teams like United and Phoenix stay competitive at all times whereas the eventual champion Kings have late runs. So does consistency matter, it would appear not.

How to Explore: Click a Franchise badge directly in the legend to filter out background noise and isolate its specific path trajectory across the competitive timeline.

The Travel Tax Vector

Proportional markers map severe geographic isolation across the league's layout. The structural distance separating the Perth Wildcats in Western Australia from the New Zealand Breakers in Auckland reveals an elite physical travel penalty that presents unique logistical challenges.

Evaluating stadium asset capacity exposes structural resource divides. Metropolitan teams play out of major entertainment hubs, highlighted by the Sydney Kings at Qudos Bank Arena (18,000 capacity) and the Brisbane Entertainment Centre (14,500 capacity). Conversely, regional setups operate under strict venue constraints, with the Cairns Convention Centre checking in at 5,300 seats and the Tasmania JackJumpers managing a 4,300 capacity limit at MyState Bank Arena.

How to Explore: Hover over individual regional markers to extract precise stadium names and cross-reference localized home crowd capacities.
Chapter 03

Franchise Production & Efficiency Dynamics

Efficiency & Margin Blueprint

Points Per Possession is a unique stat that measures both the ability of a team to dominate possessions but also to dominate in those possessions. Offensive PPP is a derived measure of how well a team can score from possession whereas defensive PPP is how well they can stifle opposition scoring capability.

Again Sydney tops the charts with its high offensive PPP and the highest PPP differential, a clear indicator of balance. Teams like Brisbane however have a net negative PPP, reflected clearly in their ladder ranking.

How to Explore: Scan the length of the horizontal linking bars, wider gaps signify extreme single sided efficiency splits, while overlapping nodes isolate balanced squads.

Net Scoring Margins

Possession number are not always consistent however, with the net margin also being an effective way to visualise team dominance. Sydney is atop the charts once more, but teams such as Melbourne and Perth jumping up the ranks, signifying that they dont need possessions to win games.

Quarter-by-Quarter Scoring Dominance Matrix

This matrix sets out to track which quarters teams capitalise on, and thus which quarters are critical to season success. So is the 3rd Quarter really the winning quarter? Does fatigue catch up with everyone?

Sydney is uber consistent, dominating from whistle to whistle, whereas teams like South East Melbourne Phoenix, have big showings in the 3rd Quarter, earning them a spot in the top echelon of the ladder. Oddly Melbourne run in rules of 2, having increased efficiency in the 2nd and 4th quarter, its a momentum game after all.

How to Explore: Hover over cell intersections to view exact normalized efficiency index ratings relative to the broader league baseline.
Chapter 04

Roster Architecture & Depth Dynamics

Franchise Roster Production & Efficiency Drill-Down

This split drill down system sets to separate the best of the best high impact playmakers and scorers from the rest. Bryce Cotton stands out as the one above all, playing on the team with the second fewest overall minutes played, and one that is competitive nonetheless he still dominates leading the league in both assists and scoring. Both Sydney and Adelaide feature a high impact combo guard (high assists and high ppg), acting as the floor general and leader, ultimately resulting in high wins.

How to Explore: Click a franchise sector within the left hand donut chart to isolate individual player profiles on the production scatter plot, click the chart's negative background space to reset filters.

Roster Hierarchy Map

To explore the so called Bryce Cotton Effect eg having a high impact on a winning team, the treemap provides a method through which the impact of each player can be measured. It plots the distribution of scoring by each player against the minutes played, with large squares/rectangles representing higher responsibilities. Illawara stands out here, having a good division of scoring responsibility amongst players with regular minutes. Whereas, as the effects name would suggest, Bryce Cottons contribution not only sits far and away atop the league, but is more than the next two highest scoring impacts of his teammates combined.

How to Explore: Hover over nested blocks to reveal individual athlete usage metrics and total roster contribution weights.

Supercoach Production Density

Plotting individual player outputs per 40 minutes using normalized distribution slopes exposes different team building strategies.

Impact does not necessarily account for efficiency and effectiveness, with a number of impactful metrics not being accounted for. Supercoach points is a metric that tracks all the positive points of a players game and pits them against the negative, giving a all around gamescore. As it is a per 40 metric, it also ignores the players minutes played, an impactful roleplayer can still be critical to success afterall. Perth and South East Melbourne show more spiked graphs, indicating reliance on more reliable stars, whereas Adelaide and New Zealand have a number of consistent producers.

How to Explore: Tall spikes indicate concentrated production zones, while broad curves highlight varied point distributions.

Roster Execution Matrices: FIC vs. PIE Metrics

In order to further explore some of the less seen impacts, its useful to look at the way that different systems judge impact, PIE (Player Impact Estimate) and FIC (Floor Impact Counter. This will help to further paint a picture of whos the most valuable, regardless of systematic bias. With far fewer minutes, Javale McGee and Kendrick Davis have phenomenal FICs, suggesting they are uber impactful in the minutes they play. PIE in this context is capturing how well a player can swing a game, with Bryce Cotton again being the hallmark game closer.

How to Explore: Switch between the standalone metric tabs to analyze changes in franchise roster distributions across performance frameworks.
Chapter 05

Advanced Player Impact Suite

Paint Security Matrix

Pitting blocks directly against Advanced Rim Recovery efficiency evaluates interior frontcourt defense. This spatial layout tracks player value based on their ability to defend the basket and contest incoming shots.

The top-right quadrant isolates elite rim protectors who alter shot trajectories near the basket. Conversely, the bottom-left space reveals roster gaps where teams lack the size and physical presence to alter interior scoring attempts.

How to Explore: Adjust the interactive slider handle to filter out low-minute rotation players and focus on core frontcourt data.

Player Archetype Fingerprints (Parallel Axes Coordinate Space)

This unified coordinate framework charts performance profiles across five dimensions simultaneously. It maps multivariable output configurations, grouping distinct athlete roles by tracing individual performance lines across all five axes. This system separates high-volume floor generals from physical, defensive interior rim anchors.

How to Explore: Hover or click specific player lines to track their relative strengths and weaknesses across all five axes.

Spatial Court Density and Shot Selection Architecture

Mapping individual shot events across court coordinates highlights modern analytical strategies. Dense shot clusters near the rim and along the perimeter show an emphasis on high-efficiency zones, while the mid-range paint remains a clear low-volume dead zone.

How to Explore: Use the toggle element to isolate specific team prints and evaluate variation in court spacing strategies.

Aggregated Court Volume & Efficiency Blueprint

Grouping shot attempts into discrete spatial grid cells reveals clear offensive focal points. Darker blue hex blocks indicate elite accuracy zones right at the rim, while varying cell sizes show shot volume concentration. This cross-references efficiency against location, showing exactly where teams exploit defensive gaps.

How to Explore: Hover over binned hexagons to view absolute field goal percentage splits and shot counts within that specific court zone.

Data Provenance & Appendix

Data Sources & Processing Framework

To fulfill data transparency, replication standards, and academic design requirements, the provenance and pipeline configurations of all compiled datasets are cataloged below.

  • Individual Player Performance & Box-Score Aggregations

    Individual statistics, advanced defensive volume values, playing time configurations, and advanced Rim Recovery indexes (ARR) are extracted from the baseline datasets tracking individual player outputs.

    Extract Player Statistics Feed →
  • Franchise Efficiency Profiles & Home/Away Splits

    Organizational points per possession (PPP), cumulative net point margins, and home/away breakdown indexes are generated and tracked directly via team analytics distributions.

    Extract Franchise Standings Feed →
  • High-Fidelity Coordinate Shot Geography

    Absolute coordinate positioning matrices and detailed event-by-event shot selections are programmatically scraped via the open-source NBLr package deployed on the Comprehensive R Archive Network (CRAN). This library maps official league tracking feeds to extract exact court coordinate pairs.

  • Derived Competition Ladder Data Structures

    The cumulative round-by-round trajectory series mapped across the dynamic competition bump graph was derived by manually reconstructing week-by-week ladder positions from transactional match summaries. Real-time win/loss records, point percentage matrices, and official NBL tie-breaking criteria were sequentially aggregated to model rank variations across the 20-round schedule.
